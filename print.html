Profile

Creative, eager to learn, team player, innovative thinker.

As a passionate developer, I love turning business problems into code in a creative way. My career shows a consistent pattern: not only delivering solutions, but lifting teams to a higher level. Whether I'm defining architecture for complex systems, modernizing legacy applications, or introducing engineering practices – I ensure teams become stronger and more independent.

I'm an expert at structuring chaotic codebases, defining sustainable architectures, and introducing best practices that actually work. CQRS, SOLID, CUPID, clean architectures, DDD, Event Sourcing, Mob Programming – these aren't just buzzwords, but tools I regularly apply and love to discuss in depth.

Experience

Senior Software Engineer @ ZF

05/2024 – Present

As a Senior Software Engineer in the Franklin team, I led the development and evolution of a sophisticated rules engine and geofencing platform for ZF, a leading automotive and transport company. The platform enables users to create generic alarms for common issues such as low tire pressure and fuel theft detection, processing sensor data from hardware installed in trailers and trucks.

I architected the groundwork for rewriting the entire platform and ensured new devices could execute rules onboard. The platform is built using C# with an AST (Abstract Syntax Tree) builder that converts rules to code compatible with both legacy and new systems, operating in a high-throughput AWS environment with Kafka, Redis, proto.Actor and MongoDB.

Beyond core development, I significantly elevated engineering practices across the organization: I introduced and mentored teams on Clean Coding, Domain-Driven Design, and Test-Driven Development through multiple company-wide workshops. I streamlined the sensor onboarding process using Claude AI agents and GitHub Copilot, enabling teams to add new sensors completely automatically. I doubled overall test coverage and implemented a comprehensive BDD architecture to make tests more descriptive and maintainable. Under this approach, the rules testing suite grew from 800 to 3,500 tests in two years, ensuring platform robustness and reliability.
